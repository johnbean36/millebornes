Rules to Mille Bornes

  1. The stack of cards will be shuffled at the beginning of each game.
  2. The computer will deal 6 cards to each player at the beginning of each round.
  3. The stack of cards will then be placed at the center of the screen.
  4. The first player to play will be randomly selected by the computer.
  5. Stop, Out of Gas, Flat Tire, Accident and Speed Limit cards that are in the players hand may be played when it is their turn.
  6. There are 50-mile and 25-mile speed limit cards that when they are played against the opponent player will limit the distance cards they can play to cards under their speed limit.
  7. Hazard cards played on a player will prevent them from playing a distance cards until they play a rememdy and they play a green light card.
  8. The Remedy cards are Gasoline, which remedies the Out of Gas card; Spare Tire, which remedies the Flat Tire card; and Repair, which remedies the Accident card.
  9. End of Limit cards will rememdy a speed limit played against a player.
  10. A Safety card can be played that will provent the opponent from being able to play a hazard of that category against them. A Driving Ace safety prevents the opponent from playing an Accident hazard, an Extra Tank safety prevents the opponent from playing an Out of Gas Hazard, The Right of Way safety card removes the need to play a green light card and keeps an oppenant from being able to play a Stop hazard or any speed limit cards against them.
  11. During any round a player is limited to only being able to play two 200 mile cards
  12. You can never have more than 6 cards in your hand. Each turn you must either discard a card or play a card.
  13. The game ends when one of the players completes a trip of 1000 miles or when the stack of cards is empty.
  14. At the end of each round each player get a score based on the following factors:
  15. The distance a player traveled are added to their score
    • A player receives 400 bonus opints for completing a 1000 mile trip
    • A player receives 100 points for every safety card played
    • If a player plays all four safety cards in a round they receive 300 points
    • A player receives 300 points for completing a round without playing any 200 mile cards
    • A player receives 500 points for completing a round without the opponent being able to play any distance cards.
